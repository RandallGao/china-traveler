## What this helps you do

Use **Shanghai Metro (上海地铁)** as your default: **20+ lines**, tight headways, and **QR transit codes** (乘车码) inside **Alipay** or **WeChat** at most gates. This path fits trips where you will **ride metro several times a day**—sightseeing along lines 1/2/10, commuting between **People’s Square**, **Lujiazui**, and **West Nanjing Road**, etc.

## Step by step

1. **Save an official map offline** — Download or screenshot the **line map** from [Shanghai Metro — English service portal](https://service.shmetro.com/en/) before you go underground; long tunnels and crowds can kill signal when you most need a transfer diagram.
2. **Turn on your transit QR** — In Alipay or WeChat, enable the **Shanghai public-transit / metro QR** (wording in-app changes). Stand on the **QR reader side** of the gate; listen for the beep before you push through. If unsure, watch one local rider **tap in and tap out**.
3. **Read the terminal, not just the color** — Platforms show **line number + direction toward the terminal station**. At big swaps (**People’s Square**, **Century Avenue**, **Longyang Road**), follow **overhead signs** for the line color *and* the next line’s number—walking transfers can take **10–15 minutes**.
4. **Know your airport rail links** — **Line 2** serves **Hongqiao Railway Station** and **Hongqiao Airport T2**; the **east end** connects toward **Pudong Airport** (check the **live first/last train** times—late flights can miss the last train). Maglev from **Longyang Road** to Pudong is optional and **separate fare**.
5. **Rush hour reality** — **08:00–09:30** and **17:30–19:00** can mean **full trains** on lines 1, 2, and 9. With **heavy luggage**, consider **taxi or ride-hail** for that leg even if you love the metro.
6. **Pick the right exit** — Stations label exits **A/B/C…**; the wrong exit can put you on the **opposite side of a highway** from your hotel. Cross-check **Chinese exit labels** with **[高德 Amap](https://www.amap.com/)** (street map + your hotel pin) before you climb the stairs.

## If this fails

Use the **service center** inside the fare gates (major stations) or the **ticket office** near the entrance if you need a **physical ticket** or your QR fails. Show **destination Chinese** on your phone; staff usually handle lost tourists daily.
