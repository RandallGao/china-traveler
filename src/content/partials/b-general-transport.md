## What this helps you do

Move through **Shanghai** when **metro is not your main tool**: **late nights after the last train**, **heavy luggage**, **suburban or riverside** trips, or days when you simply prefer **taxi / ride-hail / bus** on top. Same city as the metro path—**not** a “worse” tier, just different tools.

## Step by step

1. **Install ride-hail on hotel Wi‑Fi** — **Didi** is the default for many visitors; get it from your **official app store**, add **payment**, and finish **SMS** if required ([Connectivity](../connectivity/)). **Pin hotel and landmarks in [高德地图 Amap](https://www.amap.com/)** using **Chinese characters** from the hotel card.
2. **Airport queues** — At **Pudong (PVG)** and **Hongqiao (SHA)**, use **official taxi queues** or **app pickup zones** shown on signage. Ignore solicitors who approach you in the hall; they are **not** the official queue.
3. **Taxi basics in Shanghai** — Insist on the **meter** (打表) for street hailed cabs. Keep **¥100 in small bills** for short hops; drivers appreciate **exact change** when traffic is bad. Receipt (发票) if you need one—ask before you arrive.
4. **Buses** — Many routes accept **Alipay / WeChat transit QR**; some older lines still expect **coins**. Use **Amap** to confirm **direction and terminal stop** (Chinese) before you board on long avenues.
5. **Walking the Bund and downtown** — **The Bund (外滩)** and **East Nanjing Road** are walkable but **crowded**; watch for **e-bikes on sidewalks** and **tourist scams** (“tea ceremony” invites). Cross at **signals**; jaywalking is common locally but risky for visitors who misread traffic patterns.
6. **Trains out of town** — If you connect to **HSR**, treat **Shanghai Hongqiao** and **Shanghai Main** as **mini-airports**: arrive early, read your ticket’s **Chinese station name** twice ([Rail](../rail/)).

## If this fails

**Hotel concierge** can book taxis, hail for you, or print **destination Chinese**. Late at night, **pay more for a licensed cab** from the stand or a **reputable app**—skip unmarked solicitations outside clubs or stations.
