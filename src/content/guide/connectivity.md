## What this helps you do

Decide **whether you need mobile data or voice** in China, in plain language — **without** comparing carriers, shops, or specific SIM brands (out of scope for v1). **First landing city here: Shanghai** (Pudong or Hongqiao).

## Step by step

### 1. Do you need it?

You probably need **something beyond hotel Wi‑Fi** if **any** of these are true:

- You navigate with **live maps** (in China, **[高德地图 Amap](https://www.amap.com/)** is the practical default) or **ride-hail** on the street.
- Your **bank or wallet** sends **SMS** you must read to approve charges or logins.
- You stay in touch with family through **chat apps** while you are out.

You might **not** need a local SIM if you are on a **fully guided tour with constant Wi‑Fi**, or you are comfortable staying **offline** outside hotels (rare for independent travelers).

### 2. Two path types (categories only)

These are **categories**, not product picks:

1. **On-arrival / local setup** — You buy or collect **local connectivity after landing** (SIM counter, airport kiosk, carrier store, etc.). At **Shanghai PVG or SHA**, compare **official airport retail** vs **downtown shops** on your own schedule; passport and plan rules vary by counter.
2. **Bring-your-own** — You rely on **home-carrier roaming** or a **travel eSIM / roaming package you already understand**. Trade-off is usually **cost vs convenience**; setup happens before you fly.

We **do not** name vendors, plans, or prices here — they change weekly.

## If this fails

Finish **critical app setup on hotel or café Wi‑Fi** first. If you cannot get data at all, **delay** long cross-city trips until you have **Amap offline Shanghai**, **cash**, and a **written destination in Chinese**.
