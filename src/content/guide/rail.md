## What this helps you do

Buy and ride **high-speed rail (高铁 / CRH)** using the **normal online path** most travelers aim for — and know what to do if the app path stalls, without turning the station into a second full wizard.

**Names and buttons change.** Always follow **live screens** on the official channel you use; this page is structure, not a pixel-perfect walkthrough.

## Step by step

1. **Pick your official channel** — Most foreign visitors use the **English 12306 web/app ecosystem** or a **clearly authorized partner** linked from official sources. Avoid random third-party sites that copy the 12306 logo without proof of authorization.
2. **Create or recover an account** early — Verification (email, phone, passport details) can take time. Use contact methods you can access **during the trip**.
3. **Understand ID at purchase** — You will typically book with **passport data** exactly as printed. Typos are painful at the gate.
4. **Search trains before you need them** — Popular holidays and Fridays sell out. Note **station names carefully**: many cities have **multiple stations** (e.g. “North”, “East”, “West”). If your first city is **Shanghai**, read the **Shanghai station cheat sheet** below before you pay.
5. **Pay with a method the channel accepts** — Cards, wallets, and limits **vary by channel and nationality**. If payment fails, read the on-screen error before switching methods.
6. **Know how you will board** — Most trips are **ID-based e-ticket** at gates and turnstiles; you may scan a **QR from the app** or show **passport** at a machine or counter depending on station tech that day. When unsure, follow **station staff** at the correct gate.

### Shanghai HSR station names (first city)

Triple-check ticket wording — these are **not** interchangeable:

| You may see | What it usually means |
| --- | --- |
| **上海虹桥 / Shanghai Hongqiao** | Major **HSR hub** next to **Hongqiao airport (SHA)** — huge complex; follow signs for **railway** vs **airport** |
| **上海 / Shanghai** (上海站) | Often called **Shanghai Railway Station** — closer to central **Jing’an / People’s Square** areas |
| **上海南 / Shanghai South** | **Shanghai South** — some regional and overnight trains; verify map distance to your hotel |
| **上海西 / Shanghai West** | Smaller station — fewer services; easy to confuse if you skim too fast |

**Pudong airport (PVG)** has its own ground transport; **HSR tickets** rarely say “Pudong” as a station name — you usually ride **metro, Maglev, or taxi** to a station listed above.

### Station counter / backup plan (weak fallback)

If **online purchase fails** (verification stuck, payment declined, app outage, or no seats online), **ticket counters** at major stations can sometimes sell **remaining inventory** or explain **what is wrong with your account**.

- Arrive with **time to spare**, **passport**, and **patience** — queues happen.
- This is a **human backup**, not a parallel booking wizard. If the counter cannot fix it, you may need to **change dates, class, or route**.

## If this fails

Separate the problem: **(A) account / ID**, **(B) payment**, or **(C) no seats**. **Counter staff** often clarify (A) and sometimes (C). For **refunds and changes**, follow the **rules of the channel you paid through** — screenshots of policy pages beat memory.

## Official / trusted links

The **China State Railway Group** site at `china-railway.com.cn` has repeatedly shown a **maintenance / “please visit later”** page (including paths such as `/english/` and `crc.html`), so this guide **does not link it**. For news and corporate background, search from trusted sources or use Chinese-language official pages when they load; **tickets and travel rules always flow through 12306**.
