## What this helps you do

Buy **attraction, museum, and event** tickets without the worst outcomes — **chargebacks, wrong ID, fake resellers, or impossible refunds** — in an ecosystem that runs heavily on **mini-programs** and **platform-specific rules**.

## Step by step

This appendix is **not** a purchase tutorial. Use it as a **discipline** before you pay anyone.

1. **Internalize how ticketing works here** — Venues and shows sell through **different apps and mini-programs**. **ID rules** (passport vs local ID, real-name limits) and **refund policies** vary **per show and per platform**. Assume **stricter** rules than Western aggregators.
2. **Run the checklist below for every single purchase** — even if the UI looks “official enough.” Scams and gray-market listings exist.
3. **Prefer paying inside the official flow** you verified — avoid wiring money to individuals who message you on social apps.

**Checklist before you pay**

- [ ] **Channel** — Is this the **venue’s official account**, **official ticketing mini-program**, or a **named authorized partner**? If you cannot tell, stop.
- [ ] **Refund / change rules** — Read them **in the interface** before payment. Screenshot or save the text if allowed.
- [ ] **ID match** — Does the ticket require **passport exactly as printed**, **Chinese name**, or **resident ID**? Fix spelling *before* paying.
- [ ] **Show metadata** — Correct **date, city, venue building, and door time**? Some cities have **multiple halls** with similar names.
- [ ] **Payment readiness** — Wallet balance, card limit, or foreign-card acceptance OK **right now**? Failed payments sometimes **still lock inventory** briefly.
- [ ] **Resale red flags** — “Sold out everywhere but I have PDF” is usually a scam. Walk away.

## If this fails

**Stop the payment.** Verify on the **venue’s official website**, **official phone line**, or **box office** before sending money to a third party. A slightly worse seat next week beats a lost wire transfer.
