## What this helps you do

Finish **one** mobile wallet that can handle **a real purchase** (ideally transit or a small shop) **before** you optimize every payment method in China.

**First city: Shanghai** — you will likely use the same wallet for **Shanghai Metro QR**, convenience stores, and ride-hail.

The guide stays **neutral** between Alipay and WeChat Pay — pick **one** to complete first, then use the comparison table to understand the second option later.

## Step by step

1. **Install the app you chose first** (Alipay or WeChat) from an **official app store** listing. Sign in with a method you can still access abroad (phone number, email, etc.).
2. **Start the real-name / wallet setup flow inside the app.** Screens and required documents **change often** — follow what the app asks for *today*, not blog screenshots. Foreign cards and passport flows vary by country and issuer.
3. **Link a funding source** the app accepts (foreign card, linked account, or top-up path). If a card is declined, try your **bank’s travel notice** and **3-D Secure / SMS** delivery before assuming the app is broken.
4. **Complete any security step** the app requires (SMS, face scan, PIN). If SMS does not arrive, you may need **roaming**, **local data**, or **Wi‑Fi + alternate verification** — see [Connectivity](../connectivity/).
5. **Make a deliberate small test payment** before you rely on the wallet for long trips: e.g. a **FamilyMart / Lawson / 7‑Eleven** in Shanghai, a **Shanghai Metro QR** tap-in/out, or a **low-value top-up**. Confirm the receipt inside the app.
6. **Only after that** consider setting up the *other* wallet for redundancy — treat it as optional until your first wallet is boringly reliable.

## Comparison: Alipay, WeChat Pay, cards & cash

| | When it helps | Caveats |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Alipay** | Wide **merchant and transit QR** coverage in many cities; often a smooth default for tourists who do not live inside WeChat | Foreign-card support and **verification tiers** change; some merchants still ask for a different method |
| **WeChat Pay** | Strong if you already run your life in **WeChat**; pay and chat in one stack | Same **card and verification** variability; not required if Alipay is already working for you |
| **Card (chip / contactless)** | Hotels, major chains, some larger stores | Many **small shops, stalls, and taxis** still expect **QR or cash** — carry backup |
| **Cash (CNY)** | Universal fallback; useful when apps or cards fail | Carry **smaller notes** for taxis and vendors; count change at busy counters |

## If this fails

Pay with **card or cash** for the immediate need, then retry on **stable Wi‑Fi**. If the app keeps failing, use **in-app help** or **official support channels** — your **bank** may need to allow foreign charges or SMS. This guide cannot see your account screens.
